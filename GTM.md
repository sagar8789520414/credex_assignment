# Go-To-Market

## Exact target user

**Primary:** Engineering Manager or CTO at a Series A–B startup (20–150 employees), responsible for the engineering budget, paying for 3–10 AI tool subscriptions across the team. They approved the tools one by one over 18 months and have never done a consolidated review. They feel vaguely uneasy about the AI line item but don't have a benchmark.

**Secondary:** Solo technical founder at a pre-seed or seed startup, wearing the CTO hat, paying for AI tools out of pocket or from a small runway. Every $50/month matters.

Not the target: Enterprise IT buyers (procurement process too long), individual developers paying personally (no budget authority).

## What they Google right before wanting this tool

- "how much does cursor cost for a team"
- "github copilot vs cursor for teams"
- "chatgpt team vs enterprise pricing"
- "ai tools budget startup"
- "are we overpaying for ai tools"

## Where they hang out online

- **r/ExperiencedDevs** and **r/cscareerquestions** — EMs and senior devs discussing tooling
- **r/startups** and **r/YCombinator** — founders discussing costs
- **Hacker News** — Show HN posts get traction with this audience
- **Indie Hackers** — founders tracking costs obsessively
- **YC Slack** (W/S batch channels) — direct access to the exact target user
- **Rands Leadership Slack** — engineering managers specifically
- **X/Twitter** — follow lists of YC founders, engineering leaders; they share cost-saving tools

## First 100 users in 30 days with $0 budget

**Week 1 — Seeding:**
1. Post "Show HN: I built a free AI spend auditor for startups" on Hacker News. Target the front page. The tool is genuinely useful and free — HN rewards this.
2. Post in Indie Hackers "What I Built" section with a specific example: "Found a startup paying $1,200/mo for overlapping Cursor + Copilot subscriptions."
3. DM 20 YC founders on X with a personalised note: "Hey, I built a tool that audits AI tool spend — found most Series A teams are overpaying by $300–800/mo. Free to try: [link]"

**Week 2 — Community:**
4. Post in Rands Leadership Slack #tools channel with a real example audit (anonymised).
5. Post in relevant subreddits (r/ExperiencedDevs, r/startups) — frame as "I audited my own team's AI spend and found X, built a tool to do it automatically."
6. Ask 5 people who used the tool to share it on X if they found it useful.

**Week 3–4 — Compounding:**
7. The shareable result URL is the viral loop — every user who shares their audit brings 2–5 new visitors. Optimise the share card copy.
8. Reach out to 3 tech newsletter writers (Pragmatic Engineer, TLDR Tech, Software Lead Weekly) with a "free tool for your readers" pitch.

A single email to Credex's existing customer list ("We built a free tool to audit your AI spend — here's what we found for teams like yours") converts at a much higher rate than cold outreach. This is the channel no competitor can replicate.
