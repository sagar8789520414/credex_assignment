# Unit Economics

## What a converted lead is worth to Credex

Credex sells discounted AI credits. A typical deal:
- **SMB (10–50 person team):** $2,000–$8,000 one-time credit purchase, ~30% margin = **$600–$2,400 gross profit per deal**
- **Mid-market (50–200 person team):** $10,000–$50,000 purchase, ~25% margin = **$2,500–$12,500 gross profit per deal**
- **Repeat purchase rate:** ~40% within 12 months (credits get used, teams come back)

**Blended LTV estimate:** Assuming 60% SMB / 40% mid-market mix, average deal size $6,000, 30% margin, 1.4x repeat multiplier:
- Average gross profit per converted customer = $6,000 × 0.28 × 1.4 = **~$2,350**

## CAC at each channel

| Channel | Cost | Expected conversions | CAC |

| Hacker News Show HN | $0 | 8 consultations from 500 visitors | $0 |
| Indie Hackers post | $0 | 3 consultations | $0 |
| X/Twitter DMs (20 founders) | $0 (2 hours time) | 2 consultations | ~$0 |
| Newsletter sponsorship (TLDR Tech) | $500/send | 5 consultations | $100 |
| Credex customer email blast | $0 | 15 consultations | $0 |

**Blended CAC for organic channels: ~$0 cash, ~$50 in time cost per consultation**

At $2,350 LTV and ~$50 CAC, the payback is immediate. Even at a 5% consultation → purchase conversion rate, the tool pays for itself with 1 deal.

## Conversion funnel

```
Visitors → Audit completed → Email captured → Consultation booked → Credit purchase

100%  →  30%  →  8%  →  2%  →  0.5%
```

For 10,000 monthly visitors:
- 3,000 audits completed
- 800 emails captured
- 200 consultation bookings
- 50 credit purchases
- Revenue: 50 × $6,000 × 0.28 = **$8,400/month gross profit**

## What would have to be true for $1M ARR in 18 months

$1M ARR = ~$83k/month gross profit = ~10,000 credit purchases over 18 months = ~556/month

Working backwards from the funnel above:
- Need ~111,000 monthly visitors by month 18 (556 purchases ÷ 0.5% conversion)
- That's achievable with: HN traction (5k/month), SEO on "AI tool pricing" queries (20k/month by month 12), newsletter partnerships (10k/month), Credex customer base (5k/month), word-of-mouth from shareable URLs (compounding)

**The key assumption that has to be true:** The consultation → purchase conversion rate holds at 25%+ (i.e., the tool is surfacing real savings for real buyers, not tire-kickers). This is why the audit logic being defensible matters — a tool that manufactures savings loses trust and kills the funnel.

**The key risk:** If AI tool vendors lower prices significantly (likely over 18 months), the savings opportunities shrink and the tool's value proposition weakens. Mitigation: expand to cover more tools, add the benchmark mode, and position Credex credits as a hedge against price volatility.
